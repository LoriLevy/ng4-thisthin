Playing the Fat Game

Check the Label

Sometimes food that looks lean and healthy can be laden with fat. Take this Spicy Buffalo Chicken Wrap for example. It can fool you into adding a whole day's worth of fat in a sandwich you can eat in 5 minutes!

Track Your Fat Intake

Track and tally the fat grams you consume every day, and try not to exceed your daily goal. What is a reasonable goal? If you are a woman 20 – 25 grams per day is reasonable to maintain your weight loss. If you are a guy, 28 – 35 grams per day. Remember that the less fat you consume each day, the more you will lose.

Food and Ingredients to Avoid

Foods to avoid are those that contain any kind of oil, nuts, cheese, avocados. Avoid packaged desserts, ice cream and salad dressings. Live your life as if high fructose corn syrup is poison, because it is; it is addicting, and it makes you hungry! Artificial sweeteners also make you hungry. Avoid white sugar — instead use honey, stevia or organic cane sugar as a sweetener. Do not consume fruit juices and soda – all kinds of soda (even diet soda) – should also be avoided.

Drink Water

When you are dehydrated, it is all too easy not realize or recognize it. Water, especially with powdered fiber added, makes you feel full, regulates your bowels, and has no fat and minimal calories.

Weigh Yourself in the Morning, but not Every Morning

If you weigh yourself every day, water retention could show up on the scale and destroy your motivation. Weigh yourself every 3 days, right after you get up, just before getting dressed for the day.

Be Wary of Products Disguised as Diet Food

Take this example of the ThinkThin "Diet" bar. It has almost as much fat and calories as a candy bar, and costs twice as much.

It does have more protein than a candy bar, but according to Dr. McDougall ( Maximum Weight Loss –page 46) Americans consume about eight times the protein we need daily, which taxes our kidneys to eliminate the excess, and may lead to osteoporosis and/or kidney stones.

Eat Often, Be Mindful, and Use a Small Bowl

Eating often and using a small bowl has the best benefits of keeping you feeling satisfied and never deprived.

Eat a little something every two to three hours to keep your metabolism up. The worst thing you could do is not eat all day, and then feel entitled to eat a large dinner. This is what I used to do when I would go on a diet — that and count calories. I was never successful with that, but after I learned how to eat at HealthPlus in Portland, Oregon, I lost 30 lbs. of fat and haven't see it return. It's been more than four years.

Using a small cereal or fruit bowl will help you control your portions, as long as you avoid second helpings. If it seems like it is not enough, just wait. In about 20 minutes, a hormone (called leptin) will send the signal to your brain. IF it takes your body a bit longer to send that message to your brain, retrain it by chewing slowly, and being mindful while you are eating - not texting, web-surfing, working, driving, watching tv, or anything else.

Sleep More

Michael Matthews, author of Green & Lean, states "Your body naturally burns the most fat while sleeping." To melt your fat away, consume a light protein and carb dinner with a beverage that does not contain alcohol, 3 or 4 hours before bedtime. Avoid high–sugar foods in the evening. The author states that "going to sleep with elevated insulin levels may interfere with fat loss."

Insulin, a hormone made by the pancreas, regulates the absorption of sugar and helps your body turn sugar into energy. So if you consume sugar before bedtime and don't use that energy, the insulin causes your body to store it in your fat cells.
